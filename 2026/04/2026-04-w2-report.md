### PART 2 — Geographic Analysis

### Russia
Russian operations, led by **APT28 (Forest Blizzard)**, demonstrated a massive pivot toward infrastructure-level interception. **Operation FrostArmada** involved the compromise of 18,000 MikroTik and TP-Link SOHO routers to perform DNS hijacking, allowing the capture of Microsoft 365 OAuth tokens and bypassing MFA via Adversary-in-the-Middle (AiTM) techniques. Simultaneously, the **PRISMEX** campaign targeted NATO logistics hubs in Eastern Europe using steganography-laden PNG files to deliver implants, aiming to sabotage military aid flows to Ukraine.

### China
**Storm-1175** emerged as a highly efficient threat actor, likely linked to Chinese interests, specializing in the ultra-rapid weaponization of public-facing vulnerabilities (Exchange, Ivanti) to deploy **Medusa Ransomware**. Their "Time-to-Exploit" has dropped to under 24 hours. Additionally, the **FlamingChina** actor was linked to a massive exfiltration of 10PB of data from the National Supercomputing Center in Tianjin, indicating significant internal or state-sponsored espionage activity.

### Iran
Iran-linked actors were the most aggressive this week in the ICS/OT space. **CyberAv3ngers** (Storm-0784) targeted over 4,000 exposed Rockwell Automation PLCs in the U.S. and Israel, manipulating HMI/SCADA data. **Handala** (Void Manticore) executed destructive wiper attacks against UAE infrastructure (Dubai Courts, RTA) in retaliation for geopolitical alignments, claiming the destruction of 6PB of data. Iranian actors also successfully compromised the personal emails of high-ranking U.S. officials, including FBI Director Kash Patel.

### North Korea
**UNC1069** (Lazarus-affiliated) executed a sophisticated supply chain attack against the **Axios** npm package. This breach forced **OpenAI** to rotate its macOS code-signing certificates after malformed dependencies were introduced into their GitHub Actions workflows. The operation highlights North Korea's continued focus on compromising developer environments and software integrity.

### Other / Unknown
The **Silent Ransom Group (SRG)** and **Anubis** maintained pressure on the legal and healthcare sectors, respectively. SRG notably leaked data from elite law firms like Orrick and Wood Smith after failed negotiations. In the tech sector, **UNC6783** (Mr. Raccoon) targeted BPO providers to steal Zendesk support tickets, while **ShinyHunters** continued to exploit SaaS integrators (Anodot/Snowflake) to leak Rockstar Games' analytics data.

## Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Identity-First Defense:** Traditional MFA is failing. Move toward **FIDO2/WebAuthn** (hardware keys) for all privileged accounts to stop AiTM/token theft.
2.  **OT/IT Isolation:** Air-gap or strictly segment Industrial Control Systems. Ensure PLCs are never reachable via the public internet and physical "RUN" switches are engaged to prevent remote logic changes.
3.  **Supply Chain Governance:** Implement a strict **Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)** review process. Audit third-party SDKs and plugins (npm, Obsidian, IDE extensions) before integration into production.
4.  **Zero-Hour Patching:** The 24-hour patch cycle is obsolete. Transition to **Risk Operations Centers (ROC)** that use automated controls (WAF/IPS rules) the moment an exploit is identified.

## Operational Recommendations
1.  **Axios/npm Remediation:** Immediately update Axios to **v1.15.0+** and rotate any AWS/IAM keys that may have been exposed through vulnerable applications.
2.  **Adobe Hardening:** Apply **APSB26-43** for Adobe Reader. If possible, disable JavaScript in PDF readers via GPO.
3.  **Endpoint Monitoring:** Hunt for **LucidRook** (Lua/Rust stagers) and **PHANTOMPULSE** (Obsidian plugins) using EDR rules targeting unusual parent-child process relationships (e.g., `Obsidian.exe` spawning `powershell.exe`).
4.  **Network Monitoring:** Monitor for **MQTT (Port 8883)** and **EtherNet/IP (Port 44818)** traffic originating from foreign IPs toward internal OT segments.
5.  **M365 Auditing:** Audit for new **MFA device registrations** and mailbox rules created to move "HR" or "Payroll" emails to the trash (Storm-2755).

## Conclusion
Week 2 of April 2026 confirms that the barrier to entry for high-end cyberattacks has vanished. State-sponsored groups are leveraging consumer-grade infrastructure (SOHO routers) and developer tools to achieve strategic goals. The "Risk Mass" is growing faster than human-scale security can manage. Organizations must embrace automation and phishing-resistant identity protocols to remain resilient in an era where critical vulnerabilities are arming in hours, not weeks.

Les groupes étatiques exploitent des infrastructures grand public (routeurs SOHO) et des outils de développement pour atteindre des objectifs stratégiques. La « masse de risque » croît plus vite que ce que la sécurité à échelle humaine peut gérer. Les organisations doivent adopter l'automatisation et des protocoles d'identité résistants au phishing pour rester résilientes à une époque où les vulnérabilités critiques sont armées en quelques heures, et non plus en semaines.
