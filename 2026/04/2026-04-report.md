Threat actors demonstrated extreme agility, weaponizing vulnerabilities in developer tools and AI frameworks (e.g., Marimo RCE) in under 10 hours. ### PART 2 — GEOGRAPHIC SUB-SECTIONS

### Russia
Activity was dominated by infrastructure interception and high-velocity ransomware. **APT28** executed "Operation FrostArmada," compromising 18,000 SOHO routers to conduct DNS hijacking (T1584.002) and Adversary-in-the-Middle attacks (T1557) aimed at stealing Microsoft 365 tokens. **Storm-1175** demonstrated unprecedented speed, weaponizing N-day vulnerabilities (T1190) within hours to deploy Medusa ransomware across healthcare and education sectors. **TA446** pivoted toward mobile exploitation, utilizing the "DarkSword" iOS exploit chain (T1203) against high-value diplomatic targets via spear-phishing.

### China
Chinese operations focused on strategic data exfiltration and communication platform compromise. **Salt Typhoon** successfully infiltrated US government surveillance and wiretap systems. In Southeast Asia, the **TrueChaos** campaign exploited a 0-day in the TrueConf client (T1543.003) and utilized DLL side-loading (T1574.002) to deploy the Havoc framework within government networks to facilitate long-term espionage.

### Iran
Iranian actors shifted toward cyber-kinetic sabotage. **Handala Hack** deployed destructive wipers (T1485), notably abusing Microsoft Intune (T1078.003) to wipe 80,000 devices at US medtech firm Stryker and claiming a 6-petabyte wipe of UAE government data. **Cyber Av3ngers** targeted Rockwell Automation and Unitronics PLCs (T0843) in US water utilities, while **MuddyWater** utilized blockchain-based C2 infrastructure and the CastleRAT framework for stealthy exfiltration.

### North Korea
DPRK actors effectively merged supply chain poisoning with long-term human intelligence. **UNC1069** (Sapphire Sleet) poisoned the widely used Axios NPM package (T1195.002) by compromising a maintainer via a fake Microsoft Teams lure (T1566.003). **Lazarus** (UNC4736) conducted a 6-month in-person social engineering operation at crypto conferences, eventually seizing administrative control of the Drift Protocol to exfiltrate $280M.

### Other / Unknown
**TeamPCP** emerged as a critical cloud threat, compromising 1,000+ SaaS environments—including the European Commission—by poisoning the Trivy vulnerability scanner (T1195.002) to steal AWS/GitHub tokens (T1528). **ShinyHunters** targeted Rockstar Games and McGraw Hill by exploiting third-party cloud configurations in Snowflake and Salesforce (T1199). **UAT-10608** automated the exploitation of the React2Shell flaw to harvest cloud secrets on a global scale.

Targeting was heavily influenced by the Middle East conflict and the pursuit of high-value cloud data. **Government and Defense** in the US, Israel, and the EU faced intense state-sponsored activity. **Technology and Cloud Services** were global targets for supply chain poisoning. **Healthcare** emerged as a primary victim of destructive ransomware and wiper attacks (e.g., Stryker, Signature Healthcare). ## Strategic Recommendations
- **Supply Chain Integrity:** Implement mandatory Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) and enforce "soak time" delays (e.g., 7-14 days) before updating non-critical dependencies to mitigate the impact of poisoned releases.
- **AI & Identity Hardening:** Adopt "Bring Your Own Service Account" (BYOSA) for AI agents to prevent cross-account secret exfiltration and apply strict network isolation for AI training notebooks and LLM environments.
- **MFA Modernization:** Transition privileged accounts to FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys to counter the 37x surge in OAuth and "Device Code" phishing attacks facilitated by kits like EvilTokens and VENOM.
- **OT Resilience:** For Industrial Control Systems, enforce physical "Run" mode on PLCs to block remote logic modification and maintain offline, air-gapped backups to counter state-sponsored wipers.

## Operational Recommendations
- **Immediate Patching:** Prioritize Nginx UI (CVE-2026-33032), Marimo (CVE-2026-39987), and Fortinet EMS (CVE-2026-35616) due to active exploitation.
- **Secret Rotation:** Execute a comprehensive rotation of AWS, GCP, and GitHub keys for any systems exposed to compromised Axios (v1.14.1) or Trivy (v0.69.2) versions.
- **Endpoint Monitoring:** Deploy EDR rules to detect QEMU-based virtualization used for evasion and monitor for unauthorized `Add-MpPreference` calls used to exclude malicious directories from Defender.
- **Vulnerability Triage:** Automate patch workflows for web-facing assets to achieve a <12-hour response time to match adversary velocity.

## Conclusion
April 2026 marked a watershed moment where the software supply chain and identity frameworks became the primary battlefields for both systemic cloud compromise and geopolitical sabotage. The rapid neutralization of defensive tools and the near-instant exploitation of new vulnerabilities suggest that security teams must move toward identity-centric, continuous risk management and automate response capabilities to remain resilient against modern adversaries.

## Stratégique Recommandations
- **Intégrité de la chaîne d'approvisionnement :** Mettre en œuvre un Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) obligatoire et imposer des délais de « soak time » (par exemple, 7 à 14 jours) avant de mettre à jour les dépendances non critiques afin d'atténuer l'impact des versions empoisonnées.
- **Durcissement de l'IA et de l'Identité :** Adopter le « Bring Your Own Service Account » (BYOSA) pour les agents d'IA afin d'empêcher l'exfiltration de secrets entre comptes et appliquer une isolation réseau stricte pour les notebooks d'entraînement d'IA et les environnements LLM.
- **Modernisation de la MFA :** Faire migrer les comptes privilégiés vers des clés matérielles FIDO2/WebAuthn pour contrer l'augmentation de 37 fois des attaques de phishing OAuth et « Device Code » facilitées par des kits comme EvilTokens et VENOM.
- **Résilience OT :** Pour les systèmes de contrôle industriel, imposer le mode physique « Run » sur les automates pour bloquer la modification logicielle à distance et maintenir des sauvegardes hors ligne (air-gapped) pour contrer les wipers étatiques.

## Opérationnelle Recommandations
- **Correctifs immédiats :** Prioriser Nginx UI (CVE-2026-33032), Marimo (CVE-2026-39987) et Fortinet EMS (CVE-2026-35616) en raison de leur exploitation active.
- **Rotation des secrets :** Exécuter une rotation complète des clés AWS, GCP et GitHub pour tous les systèmes exposés aux versions compromises d'Axios (v1.14.1) ou de Trivy (v0.69.2).
- **Surveillance des points de terminaison :** Déployer des règles EDR pour détecter la virtualisation basée sur QEMU utilisée pour l'évasion et surveiller les appels `Add-MpPreference` non autorisés utilisés pour exclure des répertoires malveillants de Defender.
- **Triage des vulnérabilités :** Automatiser les flux de correctifs pour les actifs exposés sur le web afin d'atteindre un temps de réponse inférieur à 12 heures, égalant ainsi la vélocité des adversaires.

## Conclusion
Avril 2026 a marqué un tournant où la chaîne d'approvisionnement logicielle et les cadres d'identité sont devenus les principaux champs de bataille pour la compromission systémique du cloud et le sabotage géopolitique. La neutralisation rapide des outils défensifs et l'exploitation quasi instantanée des nouvelles vulnérabilités suggèrent que les équipes de sécurité doivent s'orienter vers une gestion continue des risques centrée sur l'identité et automatiser les capacités de réponse pour rester résilientes face aux adversaires modernes.
