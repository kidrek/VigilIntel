## Threat Level

**🟠🟠🟠⚫⚫ 3/5 — High**

The month was defined by the industrialization of supply chain compromises and a systemic 'identity crisis.' ## Executive Summary

May 2026 witnessed a strategic pivot by both nation-state and cybercriminal actors toward infrastructure-level access and automated propagation. Key events included the 'Mini Shai-Hulud' npm worm infecting over 1.3 million developers and significant breaches in the education and government sectors (Canvas, ANTS France). Organizations must move beyond password-centric security toward phishing-resistant FIDO2 MFA and rigorous Software Composition Analysis (SCA).

## Trends & Weak Signals

**Emerging techniques:**
- Wormified supply chain attacks targeting npm/PyPI OIDC tokens
- AI-cloned voice vishing (Bluekit) for MFA bypass
- Radio signal injection (SDR) against rail and OT infrastructure
- eBPF-based rootkits for fileless persistence on Linux

**Declining techniques:**
- Traditional SMS/TOTP MFA (consistently bypassed by AiTM/vishing)
- Ransomware encryption without prior exfiltration
- Unobfuscated script-based droppers

**Geopolitical context:** Cyber operations are now a standard component of 'hybrid warfare,' synchronized with kinetic conflicts. This is evidenced by the targeting of European flood defenses and Polish water systems during regional tensions, and the weaponization of supply chains to achieve long-term strategic espionage.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Mandate FIDO2 Hardware MFA for All Administrative Accounts**
   Session hijacking by ShinyHunters and AiTM kits like Bluekit have rendered SMS/TOTP ineffective. Deploy FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys (e.g., YubiKey 5) to secure all developers, IT admins, and cloud infrastructure access, effectively neutralizing token theft risks.

2. **Enforce Hash-Based Dependency Pinning and SCA Audits**
   Given the 'wormification' of npm/PyPI by TeamPCP, organizations must move away from mutable version tags. Pin all software dependencies to specific SHA-256 hashes and implement real-time Software Composition Analysis (SCA) to detect malicious preinstall hooks before deployment.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Emergency Patching for Edge Infrastructure and Linux Kernels**
   Immediately update PAN-OS (CVE-2026-0300), cPanel (v11.136+), and Linux Kernels (6.12+) to remediate flaws under active exploitation. Disable all unauthenticated management interfaces and captive portals on internet-facing devices until patches are verified.

2. **Monitor for eBPF Loading and Unauthorized Git Hooks**
   Audit systems for unsigned eBPF programs using 'bpftool' to detect rootkits like QLNX. Additionally, scan local `.git/hooks` directories for unauthorized scripts containing external network calls, as used by Lazarus Group for InvisiFerret deployment.

## Recommandations Stratégiques

1. **Imposer le MFA matériel FIDO2 pour tous les comptes d'administration**
   Le détournement de session par ShinyHunters et les kits AiTM comme Bluekit ont rendu le SMS/TOTP inefficace. Déployez des clés matérielles FIDO2/WebAuthn pour sécuriser les développeurs et administrateurs IT, neutralisant le vol de jetons.

2. **Imposer le figage des dépendances par hash et des audits SCA**
   Face à la 'vermification' d'npm/PyPI par TeamPCP, les organisations doivent abandonner les tags de version mutables. Fixez toutes les dépendances sur des hashs SHA-256 et implémentez une analyse SCA en temps réel pour détecter les hooks malveillants.

## Recommandations Opérationnelles

1. **Mise à jour d'urgence des infrastructures périmétriques et noyaux Linux**
   Mettez à jour PAN-OS (CVE-2026-0300), cPanel (v11.136+) et les noyaux Linux (6.12+) pour corriger les failles exploitées. Désactivez les interfaces de gestion non authentifiées et les portails captifs sur les équipements exposés.

2. **Surveiller le chargement eBPF et les hooks Git non autorisés**
   Auditez les programmes eBPF non signés avec 'bpftool' pour détecter les rootkits. Scannez les répertoires `.git/hooks` pour détecter des scripts non autorisés effectuant des appels réseau externes, utilisés par le groupe Lazarus.
