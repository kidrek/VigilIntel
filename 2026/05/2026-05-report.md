## Executive Summary

May 2026 was defined by a systemic assault on technological and sovereign infrastructure. The primary trend was the 'left-shift' of supply chain attacks, with TeamPCP compromising AI labs like OpenAI and Mistral AI through malicious library dependencies. Simultaneously, identity-based attacks matured, with sophisticated session-hijacking tools bypassing traditional MFA. The period also saw the return of high-impact networking zero-days and the discovery of specialized memory-resident malware (Fast16) targeting physical defense simulations. Organizations must move beyond password-based security toward phishing-resistant hardware MFA and immutable software supply chain practices.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Enforce FIDO2 Hardware MFA for All Developers**
   Traditional MFA (SMS/TOTP) is ineffective against the session-hijacking tools used by TeamPCP and ShinyHunters this period. Deploy FIDO2 hardware keys (YubiKey 5) to secure CI/CD pipelines and administrative cloud consoles. Mandatory revocation of all active sessions is required upon rollout.

2. **Implement Zero-Trust for Software Supply Chain**
   The 'wormification' of npm/PyPI registries requires moving to an immutable dependency model. Use Sigstore for signature verification and Pin dependencies to specific SHA-256 hashes rather than version tags in all production environments. Block all preinstall/postinstall hooks in CI/CD by default.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Apply Emergency Patches for Cisco, PAN-OS, and Exchange**
   Immediately update Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN to remediate CVE-2026-20182 and MS Exchange to apply the May 2026 cumulative updates. These vulnerabilities allow full fabric takeover and session hijacking and are under active exploitation by state actors.

2. **Audit and Monitor eBPF and NATS Traffic**
   Kazuar and KeyHunter malwares use eBPF for persistence and NATS (port 14222) for C2. Use 'bpftool' to audit loaded programs on Linux servers and configure firewall alerts for non-authorized NATS protocol traffic originating from production nodes.
