## Trends & Weak Signals

**Emerging techniques:**
- AI-assisted voice cloning (vishing) using the Bluekit framework
- Poisoning of Bun and Node.js runtimes via 'wormified' supply chain scripts
- Abuse of Git hooks for silent persistent access on developer workstations

**Declining techniques:**
- Traditional SMS-based MFA effectiveness
- Simple brute-force without AiTM interception

**Geopolitical context:** Cyber operations are increasingly synchronized with regional tensions, particularly in the Middle East and the South China Sea, where attacks against ICS/OT and transport infrastructure act as force multipliers.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Enforce FIDO2 Hardware MFA for Privileged Accounts**
   Traditional TOTP and SMS MFA are being systematically bypassed by AiTM kits like EvilTokens and ConsentFix v3. Mandate FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys (e.g., YubiKey 5) for all administrative and developer access to cloud and CI/CD environments.

2. **Implement Mandatory SBOM and Package Signing**
   The rise of Shai-Hulud and Lazarus supply chain attacks requires verified software components. Enforce Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) audits for all third-party dependencies and utilize signing tools like Sigstore to ensure package integrity.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Immediate Patching of cPanel and Linux Kernel**
   Prioritize updates for CVE-2026-41940 (cPanel v11.136+) and CVE-2026-31431 (Linux 6.12+). These vulnerabilities are actively exploited in the wild for remote root access and local privilege escalation.

2. **Disable Unsigned Git Hooks and Monitor npm Scripts**
   Lazarus and TeamPCP are weaponizing .git/hooks and npm preinstall scripts. Configure EDR rules to block unauthorized modifications to Git configurations and use the --ignore-scripts flag during development builds.

## Recommandations Stratégiques

1. **Imposer le MFA matériel FIDO2 pour les comptes privilégiés**
   Le MFA par TOTP et SMS est systématiquement contourné par des kits AiTM comme EvilTokens. Imposez des clés matérielles FIDO2/WebAuthn pour tous les accès administrateurs et développeurs.

2. **Implémenter le SBOM obligatoire et la signature de paquets**
   La montée des attaques supply chain comme Shai-Hulud nécessite des composants vérifiés. Imposez des audits SBOM et utilisez des outils comme Sigstore pour garantir l'intégrité des paquets.

## Recommandations Opérationnelles

1. **Correctif immédiat pour cPanel et le noyau Linux**
   Priorisez les mises à jour pour CVE-2026-41940 (cPanel v11.136+) et CVE-2026-31431 (Linux 6.12+). Ces failles sont exploitées pour des accès root à distance.

2. **Désactiver les hooks Git non signés et surveiller npm**
   Lazarus et TeamPCP utilisent les hooks Git et scripts npm preinstall. Configurez l'EDR pour bloquer les modifications non autorisées et utilisez le flag --ignore-scripts.
