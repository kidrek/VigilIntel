## Executive Summary

The threat landscape in May 2026 was dominated by a convergence of high-velocity automated exploitation and sophisticated identity-based attacks. The vulnerability landscape was marked by three major 'CISA KEV' additions affecting Linux and perimeter gateways, necessitating immediate patching. Organizations are urged to shift toward FIDO2 hardware authentication to counter the widespread success of AI-driven session hijacking and AitM phishing kits.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Enforce FIDO2 Hardware MFA for Privileged Accounts**
   Traditional MFA is bypassed by WebSocket overlays and session hijacking seen in TCLBanker and Bluekit campaigns. Deploy FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys (YubiKey 5) for all accounts with access to cloud infrastructure and CI/CD pipelines to mitigate token theft risks.

2. **Implement Linux Kernel Integrity and eBPF Monitoring**
   Standard EDR is bypassed by rootkits like QLNX and 'Dirty Frag' (CVE-2026-43284). Deploy kernel-level monitoring (Tetragon, Falco) to audit bpf() system calls and restrict unsigned module loading. Reference current exploits used by state actors against perimeter Linux systems.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Immediate Patching of cPanel, PAN-OS, and Linux Kernel**
   Update cPanel to v11.136.0.9+, PAN-OS to remediate CVE-2026-0300, and Linux Kernels to 6.12+. These are under active exploitation by ransomware groups (Sorry, Everest) and state clusters for initial entry and root takeover.

2. **Audit Git Hooks and Browser Extensions Monthly**
   InvisiFerret and Claude-based hijacking utilize local .git/hooks and rogue AI browser extensions. Use MDM (Jamf, Intune) to enforce an extension allow-list and scan repositories for unauthorized scripts containing curl/wget commands in the .git directory.

## Recommandations Stratégiques

1. **Imposer la MFA matérielle FIDO2 pour les comptes privilégiés**
   La MFA traditionnelle est contournée par les overlays WebSocket et le vol de session (TCLBanker, Bluekit). Déployez des clés matérielles FIDO2 (YubiKey 5) pour tous les accès cloud et CI/CD afin d'atténuer les risques de vol de jetons.

## Recommandations Opérationnelles

1. **Mise à jour immédiate de cPanel, PAN-OS et du noyau Linux**
   Mettez à jour cPanel vers v11.136.0.9+, PAN-OS pour corriger CVE-2026-0300, et les noyaux Linux vers 6.12+. Ces failles sont exploitées par des groupes de ransomware et des clusters étatiques pour des prises de contrôle root.

2. **Auditer mensuellement les Hooks Git et les extensions de navigateur**
   InvisiFerret et le détournement via Claude utilisent des hooks .git locaux et des extensions IA malveillantes. Utilisez un MDM pour imposer une liste blanche d'extensions et scannez les dépôts pour détecter des scripts non autorisés.
