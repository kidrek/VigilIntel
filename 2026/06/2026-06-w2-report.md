## Trends & Weak Signals

**Emerging techniques:**
- Vishing (voice phishing) and callback phishing to deploy RMM tools
- Hijacking Smart TVs and IoT via hidden SDKs for residential proxy networks
- Pure data-exfiltration extortion without encrypting victim files (ransomware-free)
- Fast Flux DNS hosted on residential proxies for cybercriminal C2 infrastructure resilience

**Declining techniques:**
- Traditional double-extortion ransomware with file encryption (shifting to pure data-theft extortion)
- Standard phishing email attachments without social engineering or telephone backup calls

**Geopolitical context:** State-sponsored cyberespionage campaigns targeting Russian government officials' mobile devices emphasize the persistent value of high-level intelligence. Concurrently, economically motivated actors like Silent Ransom Group and ShinyHunters disproportionately target US legal and healthcare entities, extracting highly confidential corporate secrets and personal data for strategic extortion leverage.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Implement FIDO2 Phishing-Resistant MFA**
   Given that ShinyHunters and Silent Ransom Group successfully bypass legacy MFA using advanced vishing and credential harvesting, organizations must transition to FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys (e.g., YubiKey 5). This mitigates credential-theft and session-hijacking risks across critical identity provider portals such as Okta and Microsoft 365, securing access to cloud infrastructure.

2. **Enforce Software Whitelisting for RMM**
   The Silent Ransom Group actively abuses legitimate commercial Remote Monitoring and Management (RMM) platforms like AnyDesk, Zoho Assist, and Bomgar to gain persistent access. Organizations must enforce strict software restriction policies using Windows Defender Application Control (WDAC) or AppLocker to block unapproved RMM tools. This prevents attackers from executing administrative binaries in user space.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Deploy SIEM and EDR Rules for RMM Detection**
   Configure EDR platforms (CrowdStrike, Defender for Endpoint) to alert on processes like 'anydesk.exe', 'zohoassist.exe', and 'rclone.exe' spawning from user directories like %temp% or %appdata%. This immediate visibility allows SOC teams to detect Silent Ransom Group activities and isolate compromised hosts before exfiltration begins.

2. **Isolate IoT and Smart TVs on Dedicated VLANs**
   Due to emerging SDK-based proxy hijacking on consumer devices, network administrators must isolate all Smart TVs and conferencing IoT devices on a dedicated 'Visitor' VLAN. Implement firewall rules to block outbound SOCKS and HTTP traffic to non-standard ports from these segments to prevent them from acting as residential proxy nodes.

## Recommandations Stratégiques

1. **Déployer le MFA FIDO2 résistant au phishing**
   Étant donné que ShinyHunters et le Silent Ransom Group contournent le MFA traditionnel par vishing et vol de session, les organisations doivent migrer vers des clés matérielles FIDO2/WebAuthn (ex: YubiKey 5). Cela élimine les risques de vol d'identifiants et de rejeu de session sur Okta et Microsoft 365, sécurisant l'accès au cloud.

2. **Restreindre l'utilisation des outils RMM**
   Le groupe Silent Ransom abuse activement d'outils légitimes de gestion à distance (RMM) comme AnyDesk, Zoho Assist et Bomgar. Les organisations doivent appliquer des règles de restriction logicielle strictes via Windows Defender Application Control (WDAC) ou AppLocker pour bloquer les binaires RMM non autorisés, empêchant les attaquants de s'établir.

## Recommandations Opérationnelles

1. **Détecter les outils RMM via EDR et SIEM**
   Configurez les plateformes EDR (CrowdStrike, Defender) pour lever des alertes sur des processus comme 'anydesk.exe', 'zohoassist.exe' et 'rclone.exe' lancés depuis les répertoires %temp% ou %appdata%. Cette détection permet au SOC d'isoler rapidement les hôtes ciblés par le Silent Ransom Group avant l'exfiltration.

2. **Isoler les Smart TVs sur des VLANs dédiés**
   En raison du détournement des Smart TVs via des SDK cachés en proxies résidentiels, isolez tous les téléviseurs et objets connectés sur un VLAN invité hermétique. Bloquez les flux SOCKS et HTTP sortants vers des ports non standard depuis ces segments pour empêcher tout abus de bande passante par des tiers.
