# 🇬🇧 Monthly Synthesis — June 2026

## Threat Level

**🔴🔴🔴🔴🔴 5/5 — Severe**

June 2026 was marked by highly destructive campaigns targeting critical infrastructure, the systematic compromise of software supply chains (Miasma, Mastra AI, AUR packages), and active, widespread exploitation of core perimeter security appliances. The convergence of zero-day exploits in edge devices, sophisticated defensive evasion techniques like 'Bring Your Own Hypervisor' and kernel-level BYOVD drivers, and the scale of exfiltrated data across public sector and healthcare organizations elevate the global threat landscape to Critical.

## Executive Summary

The threat landscape in June 2026 was characterized by deep systemic challenges targeting enterprise boundaries, developer ecosystems, and critical infrastructure. Edge networking devices (Palo Alto GlobalProtect, Check Point VPN, and FortiGate SSL VPN) faced relentless automated exploitation, serving as the primary initial access vector for ransomware and espionage. Concurrently, software supply chain security suffered severe disruptions, highlighted by the Miasma worm compromising Microsoft's developer environments and North Korean threat actor Sapphire Sleet poisoning the Mastra AI registry. Host-based defenses were systematically bypassed using advanced evasion tactics, such as execution within local hypervisors (QEMU) and the utilization of custom kernel-level driver terminators (GentleKiller). Geopolitically, the month saw targeted digital sabotage against public safety communications, particularly the Brazilian civil defense network and US water utilities, alongside a major shift toward risk-based regulatory prioritization via CISA's Binding Operational Directive 26-04.

## Regulatory & Legal

During this period, regulatory enforcement matured from qualitative compliance guidelines to outcome-driven, risk-based operational mandates. CISA's replacement of legacy directives with BOD 26-04 codifies a structural shift, forcing security teams to focus mitigation resources on active in-the-wild exploitation rather than relying solely on static CVSS metrics. This is accompanied by severe financial penalties for systemic privacy failures (such as the record-breaking Coupang fine in South Korea) and international law enforcement operations ('Endgame') transitioning from hosting takedowns to active device disinfection, reflecting a highly active and assertive regulatory and compliance ecosystem.

### PART 2 — Geographic Analysis

### Russia

Russian state-sponsored actors and affiliated cybercriminals focused on geopolitical espionage and financial leverage. Groups like APT29 targeted European diplomatic targets with custom backdoors, while ransomware operators such as Qilin and 'The Gentlemen' advanced their execution methods (BYOVD, hypervisor isolation) to exploit vulnerabilities in Western critical infrastructure, manufacturing, and health networks.

### China

Chinese cyber espionage syndicates maintained a stealth-first posture. Advanced groups such as Velvet Ant focused on ultra-persistent, decade-long access in air-gapped administrative networks by trojanizing PAM and OpenSSH modules, while Volt Typhoon (JDY botnet) maintained active pre-positioning and scanning across US military and maritime transport networks.

### North Korea

North Korean operations are increasingly pivoting toward automated, decentralized software supply chain compromises. State-backed entities like Sapphire Sleet targeted developer repositories and package registries (Mastra AI, npm) to harvest cloud deployment secrets and exfiltrate credentials, specifically targeting decentralized finance platforms and technology innovators.

### Iran

Iranian threat actors operated under ideological and disruptive banners. Front organizations like Handala (linked to Void Manticore) executed high-impact campaigns against critical physical sectors, compromising municipal IT and billing systems (such as US water facilities) to inflate claims of real-world operational sabotage and leverage public anxiety.

## Trends & Weak Signals

**Emerging techniques:**
- Bring Your Own Hypervisor (running Linux ransomware inside local host QEMU instances)

- Automated OAuth token hijacking for persistent multi-tenant SaaS trust subversion

**Geopolitical context:** Geopolitical friction is actively shifting cyberspace from traditional espionage to structural leverage. Concurrently, state proxy groups are actively targeting public safety utilities (as seen in Brazil and US municipal networks) to create physical operational leverage and public anxiety.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Enforce FIDO2 Hardware MFA on All Developer Accounts**
   Traditional SMS/TOTP and app-based push notifications are routinely bypassed by Adversary-in-the-Middle (AiTM) phishing and OAuth device-code session hijacking campaigns (such as Sapphire Sleet and TeamPCP activities). Mandate the deployment of FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware security keys (such as YubiKey 5 Series or Google Titan keys) for all accounts with cloud access or CI/CD console permissions. Enforce a complete OIDC/OAuth session revocation upon rollout to immediately invalidate existing stolen tokens.

2. **Implement WDAC Blocklists for Unauthorized Virtualization Softwares**
   Ransomware operators (such as Payouts King and The Gentlemen) actively deploy 'Bring Your Own Hypervisor' tactics to run Linux-based encryption engines inside lightweight QEMU virtual machines, operating outside the visibility of the host's Windows EDR. Configure Windows Defender Application Control (WDAC) or AppLocker policies to prohibit the execution of virtualization binaries (specifically qemu-system-x86_64.exe and VBoxManage.exe) on endpoints and servers not specifically authorized to host hypervisors.

## Operational Recommendations

1. **Quarantine SVG Email Attachments Containing Script Elements**
   Threat actors are bypassing secure email gateways (SEGs) by nesting dynamic ECMAScript redirection triggers inside vector graphics (SVG) attachments to direct users to credential harvesting portals. Configure secure email gateways (such as Proofpoint or Microsoft Defender for Office 365) to scan XML namespaces in attachments and quarantine SVG files containing '<script>' tags or 'onload' attributes. Set default GPO handlers for the .svg extension to static viewers.

2. **Enforce Commit SHA Pinning in Developer Configurations**
   To prevent supply chain poisoning (as seen in the Mastra AI and AUR compromises), developers must avoid floating tags and unpinned dependencies. Modify all package.json, requirements.txt, and GitHub Action workflows to pin dependencies to explicit, immutable commit hashes (SHA-256) rather than version tags (e.g., use @a4f61e... instead of @v1). Run npm installations using the '--ignore-scripts' parameter to block malicious post-install triggers.

3. **Audit and Force FortiOS Hash Migration to PBKDF2**
   The FortiBleed campaign harvested legacy FortiGate configuration backups containing weak SHA-256 password hashes to perform offline brute-forcing. Patch and upgrade all FortiGate appliances to FortiOS 7.2.11+, 7.4.8+, or 7.6.1+ immediately to enable PBKDF2 password hashing by default. Following the update, mandate a complete administrator password rotation to force the database migration to the stronger PBKDF2 structure.

## Tendances & Signaux Faibles

**Techniques émergentes :**
- Bring Your Own Hypervisor (exécution de ransomwares Linux au sein de VM locales QEMU)
- Neutralisation des agents EDR au niveau noyau par des pilotes vulnérables légitimes (BYOVD GentleKiller)
- Détournement automatisé de jetons OAuth pour subvertir les accès SaaS multi-locataires
- Campagnes de quishing (phishing QR) détournant l'infrastructure Google pour contourner les filtres d'emails

**Techniques en déclin :**
- MFA par SMS ou notifications simples sans liaison cryptographique au terminal (FIDO2)
- Stockage en clair de clés d'API et secrets AWS dans les fichiers de dépôts Git
- Déploiement massif de ransomware sans cartographie et profilage préalable de la sécurité de l'hôte

**Contexte géopolitique :** Les frictions géopolitiques transforment le cyberespace en outil de pression structurelle. Cela s'illustre par les restrictions américaines d'exportation de modèles IA (Mythos d'Anthropic) pour conserver la souveraineté sur la découverte automatisée de failles. En parallèle, des groupes affiliés ciblent des réseaux de sécurité publique (Brésil, infrastructures municipales) pour créer un impact psychologique direct.

## Recommandations Stratégiques

1. **Imposer le MFA FIDO2 Matériel pour les Développeurs**
   Les mécanismes d'authentification par SMS ou notifications mobiles sont contournés par les attaques AiTM et le vol de session OAuth (observés chez Sapphire Sleet et TeamPCP). Déployez obligatoirement des clés de sécurité matérielles FIDO2/WebAuthn (YubiKey 5 ou Google Titan) pour tous les accès cloud et consoles de build. Révoquez toutes les sessions actives OIDC/OAuth lors du déploiement pour invalider les jetons piratés.

2. **Restreindre l'Exécution d'Hyperviseurs Locaux via GPO/WDAC**
   Les groupes de rançongiciels (Payouts King, The Gentlemen) contournent la surveillance des EDR Windows en exécutant des VM locales QEMU pour chiffrer les disques depuis un environnement Linux virtuel. Configurez des règles WDAC (Windows Defender Application Control) ou AppLocker restrictives pour interdire le lancement d'exécutables de virtualisation (comme qemu-system-x86_64.exe) sur tous les serveurs et postes non autorisés.

## Recommandations Opérationnelles

1. **Filtrer les Pièces Jointes Vectorielles SVG Suspectes**
   Les attaquants contournent les passerelles email en insérant du code ECMAScript exécutable dans des pièces jointes SVG pour rediriger les utilisateurs vers des mires de phishing. Configurez votre passerelle de messagerie (Proofpoint, Defender for O365) pour analyser la structure XML et bloquer les fichiers SVG contenant des balises '<script>' ou des attributs 'onload'. Modifiez l'association système par défaut de l'extension .svg via GPO.

2. **Verrouiller les Dépendances Logicielles par Hash Commits**
   Pour contrer les attaques de supply chain logicielles (telles que Mastra AI et les paquets AUR), interdisez l'usage de tags de version flottants. Forcez les équipes à référencer les dépendances via des hashs de commit SHA uniques et immuables dans les fichiers de configuration (package.json). Exécutez systématiquement la commande npm install avec le paramètre '--ignore-scripts' pour désactiver les scripts d'installation.

3. **Migrer les Configurations VPN FortiOS vers PBKDF2**
   La campagne FortiBleed a extrait des hachages de mots de passe SHA-256 faibles dans d'anciennes configurations FortiGate pour les casser hors ligne. Mettez à jour vos équipements FortiGate vers FortiOS 7.2.11+, 7.4.8+ ou 7.6.1+. Procédez ensuite à une rotation complète des mots de passe administrateur pour forcer la réinscription des clés de sécurité avec l'algorithme robuste PBKDF2.
